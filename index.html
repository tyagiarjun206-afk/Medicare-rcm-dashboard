👥 Demographic Analysis
👴 Total Cost by Age Group
Avg total payment and out-of-pocket cost
Under-65 beneficiaries (disabled/ESRD) cost the most at $28,680/year — nearly double the 65–74 group. They represent only 15.3% of beneficiaries but are the highest-cost segment.
⚧ Gender — Cost & Events
Males vs Females — spending and healthcare use
Males spend $2,953 more per year than females ($21,663 vs $18,711) — but females have more events (82.1 vs 77.8). Male costs are driven by higher inpatient and specialist billing.
🌍 Total Cost by Race/Ethnicity
Avg total spend per beneficiary
Hispanic beneficiaries have the highest total costs ($22,580) but lowest OOP ($1,293) — indicating heavy Medicaid dual-eligibility coverage ($3,764 Medicaid avg).
💳 Medicaid Coverage by Race
Who relies most on dual-eligibility?
White beneficiaries average only $784 Medicaid vs $3,764 for Hispanic beneficiaries — reflecting stark disparities in dual-eligibility access and income levels across racial groups.

How chronic burden escalates healthcare spend
